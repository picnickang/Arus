You’re right to push back. Having read all four files in detail — no, Equipment Intelligence does not replace these pages. Here’s why:
Equipment Intelligence replaces the monitoring workflow — “is anything about to fail and what should I do?” That consolidates the risk queue from PdM Dashboard, the health scores from AI Health, the signals from Actionable Insights, and the AI chat from KB Chat into one view. That consolidation is correct and valuable.
But these four pages serve different workflows that Equipment Intelligence doesn’t touch:
PdM Pack is a manual diagnostic workbench. An engineer pastes raw vibration or pump sensor readings and gets Z-score analysis against baselines. This is a hands-on troubleshooting tool — you use it when you’re standing in the engine room with a portable vibration meter and want to check whether the reading is normal. Equipment Intelligence shows you automated predictions; PdM Pack lets you run ad-hoc manual analysis. Different workflow entirely.
PdM Dashboard has two things Equipment Intelligence doesn’t: the Schedule View (maintenance calendar/timeline planning) and the operational KPI bar (avoided downtime hours, maintenance forecast cost, asset ROI, total savings). Equipment Intelligence shows risk-sorted equipment with health scores. PdM Dashboard shows the business impact — “we saved $47K this quarter by catching 3 failures early” and “here’s what maintenance is scheduled for the next 30 days.” The risk queue portion overlaps, but the scheduling and financial views don’t.
PdM Platform is the ML engineering workbench — feature store, fleet baselines, model registry, training pipeline, inference runner, drift monitoring, prediction governance. This is for the data scientist or system admin who’s tuning models, not for the chief engineer checking equipment health. Equipment Intelligence’s “System Details” toggle shows a 5-line summary of model health; PdM Platform has 7 full tabs for managing the entire ML lifecycle. Completely different audience.
Digital Twin is physics-based simulation — expected vs observed values, residual analysis, what-if scenarios (“what happens to RUL if we run at 120% load for 30 days?”), and event replay timelines. Equipment Intelligence shows the output of predictions; Digital Twin lets you explore why the model predicts what it does and simulate alternatives. An engineer uses this to answer “should I delay the overhaul by 2 weeks?” — that’s not something the risk queue can answer.
So the actual consolidation is:
Equipment Intelligence replaces: the AI Health page, the fleet overview in Actionable Insights, the KB Chat standalone page, and the AI Sensor Audits page. Those were genuinely duplicative monitoring views.
Equipment Intelligence does NOT replace: PdM Pack (manual diagnostics), PdM Dashboard (scheduling + financial KPIs), PdM Platform (ML lifecycle), or Digital Twin (simulation). These should remain as separate pages, accessible from the Maintenance Hub and from links within Equipment Intelligence (e.g., “View in Digital Twin” button in the detail drawer, “Run Manual Analysis” link to PdM Pack).
The navigation should be restructured as: Equipment Intelligence is the primary entry point (the “what needs attention” answer). From there, you drill into PdM Dashboard for scheduling, PdM Pack for manual diagnostics, Digital Twin for simulation, and PdM Platform for model management. Four pages behind one front door instead of 16 pages at the same navigation level.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
